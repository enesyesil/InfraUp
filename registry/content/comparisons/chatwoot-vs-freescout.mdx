## The Verdict

Choose **Chatwoot** if you need omnichannel support—chat, email, and social in one inbox. Choose **FreeScout** if you only need email support and want a simple, lightweight helpdesk. Chatwoot is for customer engagement; FreeScout is for email-only helpdesk.

## Where Chatwoot Wins

Chatwoot brings everything into one place: website chat widget, email, Facebook, Twitter, WhatsApp. You get a unified inbox, canned responses, assignments, and conversation history across channels. The UX is modern and chat-first. If your support team handles multiple channels and wants a single queue, Chatwoot is built for that. It's the Intercom/Zendesk replacement.

## Where FreeScout Wins

FreeScout is a HelpScout clone—email-only, simple, and lightweight. One PHP app, one database, minimal dependencies. Perfect for teams that only do email support and don't need chat or social. Lower resource usage, faster setup, fewer moving parts. If your workflow is "customers email us, we reply," FreeScout is the straightforward choice. No Sidekiq, no Redis, no complexity.

## Bottom Line

Chatwoot for omnichannel customer engagement; FreeScout for email-only support. Don't run Chatwoot if you only need email—you're paying for complexity you won't use. Don't run FreeScout if you need chat or social—it's not built for that.
