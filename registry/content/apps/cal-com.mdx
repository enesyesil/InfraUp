## What It Actually Is

Cal.com is an open-source scheduling tool—Calendly, but self-hostable. You set your availability, create event types (30-min call, 1-hour meeting, etc.), and share a link. Recipients pick a slot, and it goes on your calendar. Supports one-on-one, round-robin, and collective availability. Replaces Calendly, Calendly alternatives, and manual back-and-forth.

## Who It's For

Consultants, sales teams, support orgs, and anyone who schedules a lot of meetings. Teams that want to own their scheduling data and avoid Calendly's pricing. Companies that need custom branding or integration with internal tools.

## Key Features

Event types define duration, buffer time, and availability. You can limit bookings per day, require questions, and add custom redirects. Calendar sync works with Google Calendar, Outlook, and CalDAV. Round-robin spreads meetings across a team. There's a public API, webhooks, and embeddable booking pages. The UI is clean and mobile-friendly.

## Honest Tradeoffs

The self-hosted version requires you to configure calendar integrations manually—Google OAuth, Outlook, etc. Each calendar provider has its own setup; it's not plug-and-play. Some advanced features (payment integration, certain apps) are cloud-only or require additional setup. The project has commercial offerings; the open-source core is complete but not every feature is free.

## Setup Gotchas

Google Calendar OAuth is the most common pain point. You need to create credentials in Google Cloud Console, set redirect URIs correctly, and configure the right scopes. A wrong redirect URI or missing scope will fail silently. Make sure your `NEXTAUTH_URL` matches your actual domain. Database migrations run on first start—give it time.

## Compared To Alternatives

**Calendly** is hosted and easier to set up but locks you in and costs more. **Open source alternatives** like Cal.com are fewer—most scheduling tools are SaaS. Cal.com is the main self-hosted option with a mature feature set. The tradeoff is setup complexity for ownership and cost control.

## Real World Fit

Cal.com fits teams that schedule many external meetings and want to self-host. Best for 1–50 person orgs with someone who can handle OAuth and calendar setup. Not ideal if you want zero-config—you'll spend an hour on Google/Outlook integration. Once running, it's solid and flexible.
